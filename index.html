Four Intelligence Layers — Built Once, Queried at Speed

Layer 1 — Cultural Foundation

8 cultural dimensions (directness, trust architecture, hierarchy, time orientation, persuasion logic, conflict style, identity frame, decision pace) with dual professional and personal interpretations. Anchored by Hofstede scores. Three-tier inheritance: East/West → Regional → Country.

Layer 2 — Contextual Intelligence

Digital communication norms (email tone, preferred channels, response expectations, marketing copy style, visual preferences, CTA patterns) and offline behavioral intelligence (greetings, meetings, negotiations, dining, gift-giving, relationship building).

Layer 3 — Relationship Intelligence

Per-contact behavioral baselines established first: response time, formality score, warmth score, initiation ratio — each with culturally calibrated thresholds. A 40% response time increase means something different from a Japanese CFO than a Brazilian sales director. The baseline must exist before drift can be measured.

Layer 4 — Scoring Rules

Machine-readable rules stored in vector DB for sub-500ms retrieval. Three rule sets per country: email scoring rules (detectable text signals → RYG severity), video behavioral rules (facial, vocal, body language, silence, gaze), and relationship drift rules — applied against the baselines established in Layer 3.
